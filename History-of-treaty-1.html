How Treaty 1 Affects Communities Today

This page explains the ongoing impact of Treaty 1, including economic development, urban reserves, and cultural preservation for First Nations communities in Manitoba.

Urban Reserves and Economic Development

Urban reserves provide opportunities for First Nations to develop businesses and community spaces within cities. They contribute to economic growth, employment, and cultural preservation.

  • The Kapyong Urban Reserve in Winnipeg creates housing, business, and community opportunities.
  • Long Plain First Nation operates gas stations and other service businesses to support their members.
  • Other reserves have developed stores, offices, and community centres, generating local employment and economic activity.

Supporting Treaty Communities

Governments and organizations continue to work with Treaty 1 communities to support cultural preservation, education, and community development.

  • Preserve cultural traditions and languages
  • Support leadership and governance in First Nations
  • Promote education, healthcare, and community programs

These efforts ensure that Treaty 1 remains a living agreement that continues to shape relationships today.

First Nations Communities Involved

The following communities continue to live within Treaty 1 territory:

  • Brokenhead Ojibway Nation — Anishinaabe
  • Long Plain First Nation — Anishinaabe
  • Peguis First Nation — Anishinaabe
  • Roseau River Anishinabe First Nation — Anishinaabe
  • Sagkeeng First Nation — Anishinaabe
  • Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation — Anishinaabe
